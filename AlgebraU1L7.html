Algebraic Expressions

Algebraic expressions can have multiple terms.

1

Monomial

7x

2

Binomial

3x + y

3

Trinomial

x2+3x+5

4

Polynomial

x3+2x2+3x-5

Terms that have the same variables and powers are "like terms" and can be combined to simplify an expression. The numbers in front of the variables are called coefficients, which are used in the operations.

Example 1

Simplify 5x2-3x+6x2+4x-6

5x2 and 6x2 have the same variables and powers and are therefore "like terms". Thus they combine to form 11x2.

-3x and 4x are "like terms" and therefore combine to form 1x, which can be written as just x.

-6 is simply a plain integer, and there are no other integers in this expression to which it can combine.

Hence, the simplified expression is 11x2+x-6

Example 2 : Use the distributive property and simplify.

Simplify 5(3x2-2x)-2(x2+3x)

5x2 and 6x2 have the same variables and powers and are therefore "like terms". Thus they combine to form 11x2.

15x2-10x-2x2-6x Next, distribute and identify "like terms".

15x2-2x2-10x-6x Next Combine "like terms".

13x2-16x The totally simplified expression.

Note:

When using the distributive property, not only are you multiplying the number in front of the parentheses to each term within the parentheses, but you must ALSO make sure to use the sign of that number.

In Example 2 above, we multiplied a positive 5 through the first set of parentheses, but then we multiplied a negative 2 through the second set of parentheses.

Summary

In this lesson, you have covered:

  • That the distributive property states that for any number a, b, and c: a(b + c)= ab + ac.
  • That algebraic expressions can be monomials (1 term), binomials (2 terms), trinomials(3 terms), or polynomials (4 or more terms).
  • That expressions with "like terms" have the same variables and exponents and they can be combined by adding and subtracting them.
